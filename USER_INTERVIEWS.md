# User Interviews

Three conversations conducted via Reddit DM and 
in-person — May 2026. All three currently pay for 
at least one AI tool.

---

## Interview 1 — str1vo, Developer, Reddit

**Date:** 2026-05-13
**How contacted:** Reddit DM after r/startups post
**Background:** Solo developer, 2 years on Reddit,
active in programming communities

**Tools they use:**
Claude — pays for Max plan (~$100/mo)

**How they decided on their plan:**
"I do extreme coding so I needed the max plan."
Chose purely based on usage intensity, no comparison
shopping.

**Have they ever questioned their spend:**
"Yes" — acknowledged they have wondered if it is
worth it but have not acted on it.

**What would make them trust an audit tool:**
"Not sure" — no strong opinion. Does not currently
have a framework for evaluating tool value.

**Current annoyances:**
"None atm" — not actively frustrated, which suggests
the pain is invisible rather than absent.

**Direct quotes:**
- "I do extreme coding so i needed the max plan"
- "Yes" (when asked if they questioned overpaying)
- "Not sure" (on what would make them trust a tool)

**Most surprising thing they said:**
They chose Claude Max purely on a feeling of needing
the most powerful option for heavy coding — not
because they hit Pro limits or compared plans. They
did not know Max is $100/mo vs Pro at $20/mo for the
same core capability. The plan name "Max" was doing
marketing work that the pricing page was not.

**What it changed about my design:**
Added the recommended plan pill showing the exact
plan name and price on every downgrade card. A user
like str1vo needs to see "Switch to: Pro — $20/seat"
not just "Downgrade" — the specific alternative and
the specific price is what makes the recommendation
actionable rather than vague.

## Interview 2 — Praveer S., Founder, AI Agency

**Date:** 2026-05-13
**How contacted:** In person — friend building an
AI-based agency that builds websites and automation
workflows for clients
**Background:** Solo founder, bootstrapped, uses AI
tools daily for client deliverables

**Tools they use and spend:**
Cursor Pro ($20/mo), ChatGPT Plus ($20/mo),
Claude Pro ($20/mo) — total ~$60/mo across 3 tools

**How they decided on their plans:**
Started with free tiers and upgraded when they hit
limits. Never compared plans across tools at the same
time — each upgrade happened independently during a
crunch period and just stayed.

**Have they ever questioned their spend:**
Yes but only vaguely. "I know I pay for three things
that probably overlap but I never sat down and looked
at it properly. It feels low enough that I do not
bother."

**What would make them trust an audit:**
"If it shows me specific numbers and tells me exactly
what to switch to, not just that I should switch. I
do not want to do more research after reading it."

**Current annoyances:**
Managing three separate billing dates and three
separate login credentials. Has accidentally let a
Cursor subscription renew while not using it for a
month.

**Direct quotes:**
- "It feels low enough that I do not bother"
- "I know they probably overlap but I never looked"
- "Tell me exactly what to switch to, not just
  that I should switch"
- "I forgot Cursor was renewing and paid for a
  month I barely used"

**Most surprising thing they said:**
He said $60/mo feels too small to bother auditing.
But when asked what his annual AI spend was he said
"about $60" — he was calculating monthly, not
annually. When I said $720/year he paused. The
framing shift from monthly to annual changed how
he perceived the problem immediately.

**What it changed about my design:**
This directly influenced making the annual saving
number prominent on the results page hero — not just
the monthly figure. $40/mo looks small. $480/year
looks worth acting on. Both are the same number.

## Interview 3 — Ashutosh, Student, College Project

**Date:** 2026-05-14
**How contacted:** In person — friend using multiple
AI tools for college assignments and side projects
**Background:** Engineering student, uses AI tools
for coding assignments, writing reports, and
research. Pays personally, not through a company.

**Tools they use and spend:**
ChatGPT Plus ($20/mo), Gemini Pro ($20/mo),
GitHub Copilot Student (free tier via GitHub
Education), occasionally Claude Free

**How they decided on their plans:**
ChatGPT Plus for GPT-4 access after the free tier
kept cutting off. Gemini Pro because someone in his
college WhatsApp group said it was better for
research papers. Has not compared them since.

**Have they ever questioned their spend:**
"Every month when I see the two charges. I always
think I should cancel one but then I use both so I
do not."

**What would make them trust an audit:**
"If my friends have used it and said it helped. Or
if it shows me a proper comparison of what each tool
is actually better at, not just the price."

**Current annoyances:**
Paying for two tools that feel like they do the same
thing. Cannot clearly articulate why he uses one over
the other for specific tasks.

**Direct quotes:**
- "Every month I think I should cancel one but
  then I use both"
- "I do not know why I use ChatGPT sometimes and
  Gemini other times, I just do"
- "Show me what each one is actually better at,
  not just which one is cheaper"
- "My college friend told me Gemini was better for
  research so I just got it"

**Most surprising thing they said:**
He was not paying $40/mo because he needed both
tools — he was paying $40/mo because cancelling
requires a decision and not cancelling requires
nothing. Inertia, not value, was keeping both
subscriptions alive.

**What it changed about my design:**
Ashutosh's case is where the "Switch Tool" action
matters most — not just "you are on the wrong plan"
but "you are paying for two tools that overlap for
your use case." I added more specific use-case
reasoning to the audit results so users understand
not just the saving but why the alternative is
sufficient for their specific workflow.
