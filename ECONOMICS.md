# Unit Economics

## What a Converted Lead Is Worth to Credex

Credex sells discounted AI credits. Average deal:
- Product: Cursor Business or Claude Team credits
- Discount vs retail: 20–40%
- Typical first purchase: $500–2,000 in credits
- Gross margin on resale: ~30%
- First purchase gross profit: $150–600

If the customer renews quarterly:
- Annual gross profit per customer: $600–2,400
- Conservative estimate: $800/customer/year

## Conversion Funnel
Landing page visits:        1,000
Audits completed:             300  (30% completion)
Emails captured:              120  (40% of completions)
High-savings leads (>$500):    30  (25% of captures)
Credex consultations booked:    6  (20% of high-savings)
Credit purchases:               3  (50% of consultations)
Revenue per purchase:        $800 gross profit
Revenue from 1,000 visitors: $2,400

Breakeven: the tool costs nothing to run at this 
scale (Supabase free tier, Gemini free tier, 
Resend free tier). Pure profit from Day 1.

## What Makes This $1M ARR in 18 Months

Required: 1,250 customers at $800/year average 
gross profit.

Path:
- Month 1–3: 50 customers from warm network 
  and organic. $40k ARR.
- Month 4–6: HN launch, Reddit traction, 
  shareable URL viral loop kicks in. 
  200 customers. $160k ARR.
- Month 7–12: SEO from shared audit URLs 
  (each URL is an indexed page with savings data), 
  word of mouth from the 40% who saved >$200/mo. 
  600 customers. $480k ARR.
- Month 13–18: Paid acquisition turns on 
  with proven LTV:CAC ratio. 
  1,250 customers. $1M ARR.

**Key assumption that must be true:**
The audit must surface real savings for at least 
30% of users. If the audit says "you're optimal" 
too often, there is no lead to convert. 
Current data from test cases suggests most teams 
with 3+ AI tools have at least one plan mismatch.

**What triggers a pivot:**
If consultation booking rate drops below 10% of 
high-savings leads, the audit recommendations are 
not defensible enough — finance-literate users are 
not agreeing with the logic. Fix the rules before 
spending on distribution.
